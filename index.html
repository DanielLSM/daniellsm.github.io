Daniel Marta

Postdoc, Learning & Adaptive Systems Group, ETH Zurich

I’m a postdoc in the Learning & Adaptive Systems Group at ETH Zurich, since September 2025. My research aims to enable autonomous systems, physical or virtual, to operate in the real world through large-scale multimodal alignment and human feedback.

My current interests stem from robotics, reinforcement learning, learning from human feedback, and generative world modeling. I envision robots and artificial agents that interact safely and adapt seamlessly to humans through diverse feedback and commands.

Before joining ETH, I was a Ph.D. student at RPL (Robotics, Perception and Learning), KTH Royal Institute of Technology, supervised by Iolanda Leite, and a research visitor at Yale University in the Interactive Machines Group. I am also a member of the WASP doctoral program.

Previously, I researched combinatorial optimization using machine learning at TU Delft in the Air Transport and Operations department, and interned at Latent Logic, a self-driving car startup later acquired by Waymo.

research interests

Reinforcement learning from feedback

Learning policies from preferences, highlights, language, rich environment feedback, and other human-centered signals.

Sample-efficient alignment

Reducing annotation and rollout burden through query selection, self-distillation, semi-supervised learning, and dense credit assignment.

Human-centered autonomy

Designing robots and agents that better align with human intent, safety perception, and interaction constraints.

Generative world modeling

Exploring how multimodal models and learned world representations can support adaptable real-world autonomous systems.
