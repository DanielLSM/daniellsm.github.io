Daniel Marta

Postdoc, Learning & Adaptive Systems Group, ETH Zurich

I’m a postdoc in the Learning & Adaptive Systems Group at ETH Zurich, supervised by Prof. Andreas Krause, since September 2025. My research aims to enable autonomous systems, physical or virtual, to operate in the real world through large-scale multimodal alignment, uncertainty-aware adaptation, and human feedback.

My current interests stem from robotics, reinforcement learning, learning from human feedback, uncertainty quantification, and generative world modeling. I envision robots and artificial agents that interact safely, know when they may be unreliable, and adapt seamlessly to humans through diverse feedback and commands.

Before joining ETH, I was a Ph.D. student at RPL (Robotics, Perception and Learning), KTH Royal Institute of Technology, and a research visitor at Yale University in the Interactive Machines Group. I am also a member of the WASP doctoral program.

Previously, I researched combinatorial optimization using machine learning at TU Delft in the Air Transport and Operations department, and interned at Latent Logic, a self-driving car startup later acquired by Waymo.

research interests

Reinforcement learning from feedback

Learning policies from preferences, highlights, language, rich environment feedback, and other human-centered signals.

Uncertainty-aware adaptation

Quantifying when VLA policies are unreliable, detecting failures, and actively selecting expert demonstrations for sample-efficient fine-tuning.

Human-centered autonomy

Designing robots and agents that better align with human intent, safety perception, and interaction constraints.

Generative world modeling

Exploring how multimodal models and learned world representations can support adaptable real-world autonomous systems.

arXiv 2026

Uncertainty Quantification for Flow-Based Vision-Language-Action Models

We introduce Velocity-Field Disagreement (VFD) for epistemic uncertainty in flow-based VLAs and SAVE, an uncertainty-guided active fine-tuning framework. On LIBERO, SAVE reduces expert data needs by at least 22% while improving failure awareness and adaptation.
